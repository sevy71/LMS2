Game Rules

Overview

Pick one team each round. If your team wins, you advance. If it loses, you’re eliminated. Teams can’t be used more than once per cycle.

Game Length & Cycles
  • Each cycle is a maximum of 20 rounds.
  • If Round 20 completes and at least one player remains active, a new cycle starts with only the active players. Those players regain all 20 teams as available.
  • If all players are eliminated in any round, a new cycle starts with all players reinstated as active and all 20 teams available.
Round Eligibility
  • A round proceeds only if every active player has at least one eligible team to pick (eligible = playing this round and not previously chosen in the current cycle).
  • Early-round choice aim: Up to Round 10 we generally target ≥3 options per player; Rounds 11–20 ≥2. In later rounds, one option may remain. This guidance won’t block a round as long as each active player has ≥1 eligible team.
Missed Picks (Auto-Pick)
  • If a player misses the deadline, an auto-pick is applied:
  • Order: (1) Walk the player’s past picks from most recent to oldest and take the opposing team (the loser in that past match) if eligible this round; if none, (2) assign the first eligible team alphabetically among teams not yet used this cycle.
  • No eligible team should not occur under round eligibility. If late fixture changes cause it, a bye may be granted at admins’ discretion.
Postponements
  • Use the round’s first kickoff time as reference.
  • Early (≥ 60 min before first kickoff): Player may re-pick; original team does not count as used. To qualify, the original pick must have been made before the official postponement announcement and at least 120 min before first kickoff.
  • Late (< 60 min before first kickoff or after kickoff): Player receives a bye to the next round; the originally picked team counts as used.
  • Same rules apply to auto-picked teams. We notify affected players and record the reason.
Special Measures

In unusual circumstances (e.g., widespread postponements), admins may apply special measures such as a universal bye, freezing or voiding a round, or overriding outcomes with a note for transparency.
