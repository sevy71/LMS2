Game Rules

Overview

Pick one team each round. If your team wins, you go through. If it loses or draws, you’re out. You can’t pick the same team more than once in a cycle. The goal is simple: be the last player left. If you’re the only active player after a round finishes, you win the game.

Game Length & Cycles
  • Each cycle has up to 20 rounds.
  • Winner: If exactly one player is still active at the end of any round, they win. A new game then starts with all players active and all 20 teams available again.
  • All out: If every player is eliminated in a round, a new game starts with all players active and all 20 teams available.
  • End of cycle: If Round 20 finishes and at least two players are still active, a new cycle starts with only those active players. They get all 20 teams available again.
Round Eligibility
  • A round only goes ahead if every active player has at least one eligible team to pick (eligible = playing this round and not already used in the current cycle).
  • For better choice early on, we aim for: up to Round 10, usually 3+ options per player; Rounds 11–20, usually 2+ options. This is guidance only—the round still goes ahead as long as everyone has at least one option.
Missed Picks (Auto-Pick)
  • If a player misses the deadline, an auto-pick is applied:
  • Order: (1) Walk the player’s past picks from most recent to oldest and take the opposing team (the loser in that past match) if eligible this round; if none, (2) assign the first eligible team alphabetically among teams not yet used this cycle.
  • No eligible team should not occur under round eligibility. If late fixture changes cause it, a bye may be granted at admins’ discretion.
Postponements
  • Use the round’s first kickoff time as reference.
  • Early (≥ 60 min before first kickoff): Player may re-pick; original team does not count as used. To qualify, the original pick must have been made before the official postponement announcement and at least 120 min before first kickoff.
  • Late (< 60 min before first kickoff or after kickoff): Player receives a bye to the next round; the originally picked team counts as used.
  • Same rules apply to auto-picked teams. We notify affected players and record the reason.
Special Measures

In unusual circumstances (e.g., widespread postponements), admins may apply special measures such as a universal bye, freezing or voiding a round, or overriding outcomes with a note for transparency.
